I wrote the following tweet today while walking from Zide Door to Fruitvale BART station: If I am more satisfied than I am unsatisfied, then I classify myself as being sufficiently happy (which is the same thing as being happy).

I deleted that tweet less than an hour after publishing it because I thought that tweet sounded like endorsing mediocrity. Rather than try to be as minimalist as I can just so that I can smile smugly and serenely about how well I practice accepting reality for what it is, I would rather allow myself to be unhappy if that is how I truly feel rather than do mental gymnastics and word play to con myself into pretending that I have no preferences and, therefore, no sense of being a unique and sentient individual (i.e. a person and not just a thing).

By the way, the strain of mushrooms I bought today was “Loving Teacher” (which is allegedly the same strain as “Golden Teacher”) for $80. On Wednesdays and Sundays, there is a 20% discount. Good to know! I will probably not go back for more shrooms until at least after Sunday so I can save up more money and get my web empire fully up to date. So far, so good. I have a general plan for how to accomplish that.

As I get more experienced with this journaling system, I hope to announce my plans less often and to make future content more information-dense, more clearly worded, and more interesting (i.e. pertaining to what I think is sufficiently worthwhile, engrossing, and satisfying to experience and to think about).
