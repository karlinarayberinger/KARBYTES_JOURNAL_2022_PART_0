Ideas Of Interest: A few days ago, I found an amazing article on the Internet about quantum computers which use quantum entangled photons to process data without needing an external clock as a reference by having each photon which passes through a beam splitter get entangled within a fixed temporal length of the last photon which went through that beam splitter such that the temporal gap between each successive photon is calibrated by the second to last photon because the previous two photons are supposedly in quantum entanglement with each other and hence forced to move in sync with each other). It seems impossible for any information processing agent to predict whether any particular photon will continue going straight through the beam splitter or else go orthagonally in the only other allowed direction. (Note that my understanding of these concepts is in its infancy and may be riddled with falsities). Depending on where a particular beam “decides” to go supposedly creates a parallel universe at the moment the photon “decides”. With each successive “decision” the same photon makes, that photon is separated from its “starting universe” by how many universes that photon was used to split. Perhaps this explains why light seems to always travel the same speed through a vacuum and without other forces imposing on it from multiple frames of reference which apparently observe the same light simultaneously. I am not sure how people measure the speed of one photon from multiple spatial locations. Perhaps each unique frame of reference has its own “copy” of the “original” photon which was “split” by going to a particular observer (i.e. frame of reference) (i.e. universe)).
