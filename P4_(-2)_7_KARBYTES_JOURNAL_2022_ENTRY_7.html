KARBYTES_JOURNAL_2022_ENTRY_7

If I find a way to relax consistently enough, then I might be able to make progress towards my goals at a rate which is sufficiently high rather than so low that it basically ends up getting neutralized by “start up costs” each time I have to recommit to the same goal.

The only way for me to relax is to make relaxation my only goal. Even that is bound to fail given my circumstances. It seems that I am physically being prevented from relaxing so that I stay chronically in “fight of flight mode” rather than happy and productive.

Apparently I am not allowed to be happier than whoever “head of household” is (especially if I am on that person’s property).

It’s okay if you can’t study computer science because those who are higher on the totem pole than you do not want you to become too smart for them to understand.

I am writing these “would be” tweets in the bathroom at my old house (i.e. my mother’s house on the west side of Castro Valley) between my old room and my brother’s old room on the second story of that two-story house. I can tell that the thought police (and many civilians) are eager to antagonize me to the point that I am forced to leave the premises and go back to scrounging around for shade to use my laptop. It’s hard to find places where I do not feel intruded on or otherwise set up to fail.

“Stop complaining or we will give you something to complain about,” seems to be the message. Yet, as soon as I stop complaining, people seem to get angry at me and, when I do not react, they start physically violating me and getting away with it.

The world I live in seems to revolve around protecting the rich at all costs. Only the rich are allowed to have nice places to study. Everyone else is “supposed to” be too deprived of adequate space, time, energy, nutrition, and other resources (especially money) to justify studying anything rigorous or “high end” such as physics or web development. The poor can be blamed for making themselves and their children poor (and everyone else poorer) by having kids when they know they cannot afford to provide a decent standard of living for such kids without having to rely on donations and government assistance to meet bare minimum needs. The rich can be blamed for there being poverty at all by not paying much higher taxes than everyone else and donating all their surplus income to charity such that those “rich people” are forced to downgrade their standard of living to be on par with those considered to be former charity cases.

As I write this note, I am slowly but surely calming down from what feels like a lifelong battle to take up space and to own my own personhood; to not settle for merely being a space which other people only see as a medium through which to transmit and to replicate their cherished memes and genes; to neither shut up nor emote merely because someone higher on the totem pole insinuates that is what they want me to do.

I only consumed two mushrooms out of the handful in my bag. I plan on doing the rest later in one sitting when my stomach empties more (probably around 10PM).

One more thing: most people look and act worse than I do because they are more willing than I am to tolerate self-abusive situations. Such people seem to think that my quality of life (and consciousness) will plummet rather than improve as time progresses because they are sold on the idea that settling for mediocrity, being raped into submission, and having few options is a mandatory rite of passage which unites all humans in a common struggle for dignity and justice rather than allowing anyone to be healthier than the average simpleton. My “job” amounts to little more than doing whatever I want at all times (as physical reality permits). I am the only human being I defer to as an authority figure. Humans who bully me into submission using brute force and threatening to harm me with weapons if I do not comply usually get my compliance because I want my personal evolution to continue rather than to end (and if it gets impeded by other people trying to slow me down or imprison me, I still see it as a challenge to generate reasons to keep living and goals to keep striving for so that I do not merely sleep my life away in an attempt to escape what some of my tormentors seem to want me to conclude: that it is impossible for me to feel that my time not sleeping is worthwhile or even bearable and that I should, hence, just sleep to avoid being so brutally tortured).

I appreciate being able to get these words out of my mind and onto this page (and then back into my mind again whenever I revisit this journal of a web page).
